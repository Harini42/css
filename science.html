Science & Technology Museums in India

Visvesvaraya Industrial & Technological Museum, Bangalore

Visvesvaraya Industrial and Technological Museum is home to many scientific exhibits and models. Opened in 1962 and inaugurated by our first Prime Minister Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru, the museum is one of its kind and was created to honor Sir M. Visvesvaraya, the bearer of India’s highest civilian honour Bharat Ratna. Today, the museum has many scientific galleries and models.

Birla Industrial & Technological Museum, Kolkata

The Birla Industrial & Technological Museum West Bengal, is one of the marvellous destinations for science and technology lovers. Established in 1959 by the Birla Foundation, it is among India’s earliest established museums of such a genre. From machine and engine models to a show on space exploration and energy, Birla Industrial & Technological Museum has several exhibits that take an exciting journey into the world of industrial development, engineering, and technological advancement.

Nehru Science Centre, Mumbai

Nehru Science Centre (NSC) is the largest interactive science centre in India. It is located in Worli, Mumbai. The centre is named after India's first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru. In 1977, the centre started with the 'Light and Sight' exhibition, and then in 1979 a Science Park was built. On 11 November 1985 it was opened to the public by Rajiv Gandhi, the then Prime Minister of India.
